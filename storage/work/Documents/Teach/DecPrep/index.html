The 5 + 5 Curriculum

Two classes per week. Use the tabs below to switch between the Math track (The Engine) and the English track (The Interface).

01 Number Systems

The Digital Foundation: Bases & Logic

Connecting high school number theory to computer science. Why do computers count differently?

  • Real, Natural, & Integer numbers visualization.
  • The Number Line & The idea of Infinity.
  • Binary (Base-2) & Hexadecimal (Base-16) intro.
Activity: Convert your birthday to Binary.
Tech Connection: How memory addresses work (Hex).
02 Growth & Decay

Exponentials & Logarithms

Understanding scale. Essential for algorithm complexity (Big O notation) later.

  • Laws of Indices/Powers.
  • Logarithms: What they actually mean (undoing powers).
  • Factorials (!) and explosive growth.
Activity: Paper folding challenge (Exponential growth).
Tech Connection: Why searching a sorted list is O(log n).
03 Data Math

Percentages, Ratios & Fractions

The math of business logic and system performance metrics.

  • Fast mental math for percentages.
  • Ratio division (allocating resources).
  • Profit/Loss basics.
Activity: Calculate download time remaining based on speed/size.
Tech Connection: CPU utilization & SLAs.
04 Combinatorics

Permutation, Combination & Sets

The logic of possibilities. Crucial for security (passwords) and databases.

  • Set Theory basics (Union, Intersection).
  • Fundamental Counting Principle.
  • Permutation vs. Combination: When order matters.
Activity: How many unique passwords can you make with 4 digits?
Tech Connection: Database queries (SQL JOINs).
05 Structures

Matrices & Basic Algebra

Handling multi-dimensional data.

  • Matrix addition, multiplication.
  • Solving simple linear equations.
  • Variables and substitution.
Activity: Represent an image as a matrix of pixels.
Tech Connection: Graphics processing & Arrays.
01 Vocabulary

The Language of IT

Building a glossary of frequent FE exam terms. Moving from "Computer" to "Architecture".

  • Keywords: Protocol, throughput, latency, redundancy.
  • Hardware vs Software verb associations.
  • Tech News: Reading a simple TechCrunch headline.
Task: Create flashcards for 20 provided IT terms.
02 Decoding

Anatomy of a Long Question

FE questions are often long scenarios. We learn to strip away the fluff.

  • Identifying the "Ask": What is the question actually wanting?
  • Subject-Verb-Object in complex technical sentences.
  • Removing adjectives to find core logic.
Task: "Sentence Surgery" - Crossing out irrelevant words in a sample question.
03 Documentation

Reading Docs & Manuals

Information extraction. Reading for data rather than for story.

  • Scanning techniques: Looking for numbers, capitalization, and bullets.
  • Understanding conditional statements (If/Else in text).
  • Reading flowcharts description.
Task: Extract specifications from a dummy "Server Setup" guide.
04 Logic Text

Algorithm Descriptions

How English is used to describe step-by-step processes.

  • Sequence words: Initial, subsequent, prior to, simultaneous.
  • Causality: Due to, resulting in, triggers.
  • Translating text to pseudo-code.
Task: Draw a simple diagram based on a text description of a network.
05 Simulation

Exam Stamina & Speed

Putting it all together. Handling pressure.

  • Time management strategies for reading.
  • Skipping difficult words without losing context.
  • Mini-Mock test (Text based).
Task: 5 questions in 10 minutes. Review and feedback.
